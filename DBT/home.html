Aadhaar-linked vs DBT-enabled Aadhaar-seeded accounts

Both use your Aadhaar, but only DBT-enabled Aadhaar-seeded accounts are ready to receive government benefits directly.

Aadhaar-linked bank account

Your bank has your Aadhaar number mapped to your customer profile for eKYC and identification.

  • Used for identity verification and eKYC.
  • May help with login, verification, or onboarding with your bank.
  • Not guaranteed to receive Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) payments.
Identity-linked only

DBT-enabled Aadhaar-seeded bank account

Your Aadhaar is seeded to the account and enabled in the NPCI mapper to receive DBT.

  • Aadhaar is seeded to a specific account number.
  • Enabled in NPCI’s Aadhaar Payment Bridge System (APBS) for DBT.
  • Ready to receive scholarships, stipends, and other government benefits.
DBT-ready

Key differences

Aadhaar-linked account

  • Aadhaar present only for identity/eKYC at the bank-level.
  • No NPCI/APBS mapping for benefits.
  • Payments via DBT may fail or not be attempted.
  • Action needed: request seeding and DBT enablement.

DBT-enabled Aadhaar-seeded account

  • Aadhaar mapped to a specific account in bank systems.
  • NPCI mapper updated; APBS routes DBT to this account.
  • Most reliable for receiving government transfers.
  • Keep account active and KYC updated to avoid rejections.

Tip: Ask your bank to confirm: “Is my Aadhaar seeded to this account and DBT-enabled in NPCI?”

Frequently Asked Questions

Visit your bank branch or mobile banking and request Aadhaar seeding for the specific account and ask to enable it for DBT/APBS. Keep your Aadhaar and passbook/debit card handy.
NPCI typically maps one active Aadhaar-seeded account per bank for DBT. Benefits are routed to the account marked active in the mapper. Confirm with your bank which account is active.
Check with your bank that your account is active, KYC is up to date, Aadhaar is seeded, and the account is DBT-enabled in NPCI. Ask for the latest APBS status.
For official details, refer to your bank and government scheme guidelines.
